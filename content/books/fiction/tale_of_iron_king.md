# Tale of the Iron King

Long ago, when the mountains had not yet known snow, a king ruled in distant lands. They called him the Iron King -- not because he was cruel, but because his will was as unyielding as the blade of his sword.

The Iron King conquered seven cities and united three peoples under his banner. His army feared no one, and his enemies bowed before the mere sound of his name. But one day, everything changed.

## The Betrayal

His closest advisor, Lord Eldric, whom the king had known since childhood, conspired with the eastern dukes. They planned to open the castle gates at night and let in enemy soldiers. But the plot was discovered by the king's young page, who overheard the traitors whispering in the garden.

When Eldric was brought before the king in chains, silence fell upon the throne room. Everyone awaited the verdict. By the laws of the kingdom, the punishment for treason was death.

## The Choice

The Iron King looked at his old friend and saw not a traitor -- but the boy with whom he once played in the castle courtyard. He remembered how Eldric saved his life in the Battle of the Northern Pass.

"Tell me," the king said, "why did you do this?"

"Because you stopped listening," Eldric replied. "You became deaf to the pain of those you rule. The eastern villages are starving, and you build new fortresses."

The throne room fell silent. And the Iron King made a decision that astonished everyone.

## The Mercy

He stripped Eldric of his titles and lands, but spared his life. He sent him to the eastern villages -- not as punishment, but as a governor who understood their pain. "If you truly care about these people," the king said, "then prove it with deeds."

A year later, the eastern provinces flourished. Eldric built irrigation channels and opened granaries. The people who once demanded war now sang songs of the Iron King's wisdom.

## The Lesson

The chronicler who recorded this story wrote at the end: "True strength lies not in the ability to punish, but in the wisdom to see beyond anger. A king who can show mercy without weakness is a king who will be remembered for centuries."

And so it was. The name of the Iron King was spoken with reverence long after his death -- not because of his conquests, but because of that one decision in the throne room.
