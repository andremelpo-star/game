# A Brief Guide to Herbs and Their Properties

This guide was compiled by the herbalist Marta from the village of Zeleny Bor. Every sage should know the basics of herbcraft -- for the people will come seeking advice not only about rulers and wars, but also about everyday ailments.

## Healing Herbs

**Silverwort** -- grows along riverbanks, blooms in early summer. A tincture of its leaves heals wounds and reduces fever. The key is not to confuse it with false silverwort, which has reddish veins on the underside of the leaf.

**Calmroot** -- found in shaded forest glades. Tea from this root soothes nerves and helps with insomnia. Useful advice for those who come with anxiety and fear.

## Dangerous Plants

**Nightshade** -- beautiful purple berries that attract children. Extremely poisonous. Even three berries can cause severe poisoning. A sage must know this to warn parents.

**Devil's Claw** -- a vine that wraps around trees. Its sap causes skin burns. However, in small doses mixed with bear fat, it relieves joint pain.

## Herbs and Weather

Experienced herbalists can predict drought by observing plants. When **dustcap** begins to bloom ahead of schedule -- expect dry times. If **marshweed** retreats from the water's edge -- the groundwater level is dropping, and drought will come in two to three weeks.

This knowledge is especially useful when farmers come seeking advice about their crops. Often what is attributed to curses is simply a natural cycle that can be predicted and prepared for.
